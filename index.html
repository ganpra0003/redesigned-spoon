"A hat is not merely clothing.
It is a state of mind."

The journey begins in the bamboo forests of Damyang. Only the finest strands, split to the width of a human hair, are chosen for their resilience and grace. It is a process that refuses to be rushed.

45 Days to Craft
0.2mm Strand Width
01

Sourcing the Elements

zoom_in Explore Texture

Location: Damyang Bamboo Forest
Material: 3-Year Aged Bamboo

We harvest only in the dead of winter, when the bamboo is most dense. The outer skin is stripped, boiled in caustic soda, and dried in the shade for months.

The Split

One stalk becomes one hundred strands.

02

The Weave (Yangtae)

grid_on

Precision Grid

Each crossing point is calculated. The grid must be uniform to allow light to pass through softly.

gesture

Horsehair Tension

Horsehair provides the structural integrity, woven horizontally against the vertical bamboo.

water_drop

Moisture Control

The weaving room is kept at high humidity to prevent the brittle bamboo from snapping.

03

Lacquering & Assembly

The final form is dipped in traditional black ink and lacquer. This not only gives the Gat its signature color but solidifies the delicate weave into a durable structure.
